MERIDIAN NATIONAL BANCORP -- CREDIT PORTFOLIO QUALITY REVIEW, FY 2023
=======================================================================

Total loans and leases held on the balance sheet were $731.4 billion as of December 31, 2023, an increase of 4.1% from $702.6 billion at year-end 2022. The following provides a detailed review of credit quality across the Company's major lending portfolios.

COMMERCIAL LOANS
Total commercial loans were $394.7 billion, representing 54.0% of the total loan portfolio. Commercial and industrial (C&I) loans of $267.3 billion grew 5.2% year-over-year, supported by demand from middle market and large corporate clients. Commercial real estate (CRE) loans of $127.4 billion grew 1.8%, as the Company maintained a cautious posture toward the office subsector. The CRE office portfolio totaled $34.6 billion, with a weighted-average loan-to-value ratio of 58.3%. Net charge-offs on commercial loans were $1.6 billion, or 0.41% of average commercial loans, compared to 0.28% in the prior year. The delinquency rate (30+ days past due) for commercial loans was 1.14%, up from 0.87%.

CONSUMER LOANS
Total consumer loans were $212.8 billion, accounting for 29.1% of the portfolio. Credit card receivables of $92.4 billion grew 7.8%, reflecting strong account acquisition and higher revolving balances. Auto loans of $61.7 billion increased 2.3%. Personal loans and other consumer credit totaled $58.7 billion. Net charge-offs on consumer loans were $2.1 billion, representing a net charge-off rate of 1.01%, compared to 0.72% in FY 2022. The 30+ day delinquency rate for consumer loans rose to 2.47% from 1.93%, driven primarily by the credit card and personal loan portfolios.

RESIDENTIAL MORTGAGE
Residential mortgage loans totaled $123.9 billion, or 16.9% of total loans. The portfolio carried a weighted-average FICO score of 748 and a weighted-average LTV of 61.2%. Net charge-offs on mortgages were $0.5 billion, or 0.40%, reflecting the portfolio's high quality. The 30+ day delinquency rate was 1.62%, compared to 1.48% in the prior year. New mortgage originations for FY 2023 were $47.3 billion, down 18.4% from $58.0 billion in 2022 as higher interest rates dampened refinancing activity.
