MERIDIAN NATIONAL BANCORP -- INVESTMENT BANKING SEGMENT REVIEW, FY 2023
=========================================================================

The Investment Banking segment reported total revenue of $12.5 billion for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2023, an increase of 1.6% from $12.3 billion in the prior year. Pre-tax income was $3.8 billion, down 2.6% year-over-year, as revenue growth was offset by higher compensation and technology costs. The segment's pre-tax margin was 30.4%, compared to 31.7% in FY 2022.

ADVISORY AND UNDERWRITING
Total investment banking fees were $4.3 billion, a decline of 3.0% from $4.4 billion in FY 2022. M&A advisory revenue was $2.1 billion, down 7.9% from $2.3 billion, reflecting a 12.4% decline in global announced M&A deal volume during the year. The Company advised on 184 completed transactions with an aggregate deal value of $297 billion. Equity underwriting revenue was $0.9 billion, up 14.3% from $0.8 billion, as IPO markets showed signs of recovery in the second half of the year. The Company served as bookrunner on 67 equity offerings. Debt underwriting revenue of $1.3 billion was flat year-over-year, as investment-grade issuance offset weakness in leveraged finance.

MARKETS AND TRADING
Total markets revenue was $8.2 billion, an increase of 4.1% from $7.9 billion in FY 2022. Fixed income, currencies, and commodities (FICC) trading revenue was $5.4 billion, up 6.3%, driven by strong client activity in rates and credit products amid elevated volatility. Equities trading revenue was $2.8 billion, flat year-over-year, with growth in derivatives offset by lower cash equities volumes. Prime brokerage balances averaged $214 billion during the year.

MARKET CONDITIONS AND OUTLOOK
Global fee pools for investment banking were estimated at $78.2 billion in 2023, down approximately 4% from 2022. Meridian maintained a market share of approximately 5.5% in global IB fees. The pipeline of announced but not yet closed M&A transactions was $83 billion as of year-end, suggesting improved fee realization in early 2024. Management expects capital markets activity to normalize as rate uncertainty diminishes.
