MERIDIAN NATIONAL BANCORP -- ANNUAL REPORT FISCAL YEAR 2023
===========================================================

Meridian National Bancorp ("the Company") reported consolidated net revenue of $48.7 billion for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2023, representing an increase of 6.2% compared to $45.9 billion in the prior year. Net income attributable to common shareholders was $13.4 billion, or $9.82 per diluted share, compared to $12.1 billion, or $8.67 per diluted share, in fiscal year 2022.

The improvement in financial performance was driven primarily by higher net interest income, which increased 11.3% to $29.6 billion, benefiting from a favorable rate environment and disciplined balance sheet management. Noninterest revenue of $19.1 billion declined 1.4% year-over-year, reflecting subdued capital markets activity and lower mortgage banking income, partially offset by growth in wealth management fees.

Return on average common equity (ROE) was 14.8%, compared to 13.5% in the prior year. Return on average assets (ROA) was 1.12%, up from 1.04%. The efficiency ratio improved to 57.3% from 59.1%, reflecting the Company's ongoing focus on operational discipline and technology-led productivity gains.

Total assets as of December 31, 2023 stood at $1.24 trillion, an increase of 3.8% from $1.19 trillion at the end of 2022. Total deposits were $934 billion, reflecting modest deposit attrition of 1.2% as customers migrated balances into higher-yielding alternatives.

Key challenges during the fiscal year included persistent deposit repricing pressure, a normalization of credit costs from historically low levels, and elevated regulatory scrutiny. The provision for credit losses increased to $5.8 billion from $3.9 billion in 2022, reflecting a deterioration in the macroeconomic outlook and seasoning within the consumer credit portfolio.

The Company returned $9.7 billion to shareholders through dividends of $5.2 billion and share repurchases of $4.5 billion. The Board of Directors approved a new $6.0 billion share repurchase authorization effective January 2024.

Management remains focused on prudent growth, disciplined expense management, and maintaining fortress-level capital and liquidity positions as the Company navigates an uncertain economic environment heading into 2024.
