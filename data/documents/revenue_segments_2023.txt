MERIDIAN NATIONAL BANCORP -- REVENUE BY BUSINESS SEGMENT, FY 2023
===================================================================

The Company operates through three principal business segments: Consumer Banking, Commercial Banking, and Investment Banking. The following provides a summary of each segment's financial performance for the year ended December 31, 2023.

CONSUMER BANKING
Total segment revenue was $21.3 billion, an increase of 9.7% from $19.4 billion in 2022. Net interest income of $15.8 billion grew 14.2%, driven by higher card and auto loan yields. Noninterest revenue of $5.5 billion was relatively flat, as higher deposit service charges offset lower mortgage origination fees. The segment contributed 43.7% of consolidated revenue. Pre-tax income was $7.1 billion, up 12.4% year-over-year. The consumer banking segment benefited from strong credit card spend volumes, which increased 8.3% to $412 billion, and a 6.1% increase in average deposit balances to $487 billion.

COMMERCIAL BANKING
Total segment revenue was $14.9 billion, representing growth of 5.1% from $14.2 billion in the prior year. Net interest income of $10.4 billion rose 7.8%, supported by loan growth of 4.3% and wider lending spreads. Noninterest revenue of $4.5 billion was down 1.9%, driven by lower treasury services fees. The segment accounted for 30.6% of consolidated revenue. Pre-tax income of $5.6 billion increased 3.2%, with the provision for credit losses rising to $1.4 billion from $0.9 billion due to downgrades in the commercial real estate portfolio.

INVESTMENT BANKING
Total segment revenue was $12.5 billion, an increase of 1.6% from $12.3 billion in fiscal year 2022. Markets revenue of $8.2 billion was up 4.1%, led by strong fixed income trading. Advisory and underwriting fees of $4.3 billion declined 3.0% from $4.4 billion, reflecting lower M&A deal volumes globally. The segment represented 25.7% of total revenue. Pre-tax income was $3.8 billion, down 2.6% year-over-year, as higher compensation costs and technology investments weighed on margins. Compensation-to-revenue ratio for the segment was 33.4%.
