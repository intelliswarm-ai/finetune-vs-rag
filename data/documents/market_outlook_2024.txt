MERIDIAN NATIONAL BANCORP -- FORWARD-LOOKING GUIDANCE AND MARKET OUTLOOK, FY 2024
====================================================================================

The following forward-looking statements reflect management's expectations as of January 18, 2024 and are subject to significant uncertainty. Actual results may differ materially from these projections.

REVENUE OUTLOOK
Management expects consolidated net revenue for FY 2024 in the range of $49.5 billion to $51.0 billion, representing growth of approximately 2% to 5% year-over-year. This outlook assumes the Federal Reserve begins reducing the federal funds rate in mid-2024, with cumulative rate cuts of 75 to 100 basis points by year-end. Net interest income is expected to be $28.5 billion to $29.5 billion, reflecting modest NIM compression of 5 to 15 basis points as deposit repricing continues and lower rates reduce asset yields. Noninterest revenue is projected at $21.0 billion to $21.5 billion, supported by a recovery in capital markets activity and continued growth in wealth management.

CREDIT COST PROJECTIONS
The provision for credit losses is expected to be in the range of $6.0 billion to $7.0 billion for FY 2024, reflecting continued normalization of consumer credit losses and potential stress in the commercial real estate sector. Net charge-offs are projected at 0.60% to 0.75% of average loans. The allowance for credit losses is expected to remain at or above 2.00% of total loans.

EFFICIENCY AND EXPENSE TARGETS
Noninterest expense is expected to be approximately $28.5 billion to $29.0 billion, with the efficiency ratio targeted at 56% to 58%. Headcount is projected to decline by approximately 3,000 to 4,000 full-time equivalents through attrition and continued automation.

CAPITAL MANAGEMENT
The Company expects to maintain a CET1 ratio of 12.5% to 13.0%, reflecting the impact of proposed Basel III endgame rules and organic RWA growth. Total capital returns to shareholders are expected to be $10.0 billion to $11.0 billion, consisting of approximately $5.5 billion in dividends and $4.5 billion to $5.5 billion in share repurchases, subject to Board approval and market conditions.

ECONOMIC ASSUMPTIONS
The guidance assumes U.S. GDP growth of 1.5% to 2.0%, unemployment of 4.2% to 4.6%, and no recession during FY 2024.
