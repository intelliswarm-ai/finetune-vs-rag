MERIDIAN NATIONAL BANCORP -- NET INTEREST INCOME AND RATE SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS
=================================================================================

Net interest income (NII) for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2023 was $29.6 billion, an increase of 11.3% from $26.6 billion in FY 2022. The net interest margin (NIM) expanded to 2.68% from 2.49%, an improvement of 19 basis points year-over-year.

DRIVERS OF NII GROWTH
The increase in net interest income was attributable to the following factors: (i) higher benchmark interest rates, which contributed approximately $2.4 billion of incremental NII, as the Federal Reserve maintained the federal funds rate target range at 5.25%-5.50% for much of the year; (ii) loan growth of 4.1%, which contributed approximately $0.9 billion; partially offset by (iii) higher deposit costs, which increased by $1.3 billion as the average cost of interest-bearing deposits rose 82 basis points to 2.74% from 1.92%.

NET INTEREST MARGIN TRENDS
NIM by quarter was as follows: Q1 2023: 2.58%; Q2 2023: 2.71%; Q3 2023: 2.74%; Q4 2023: 2.67%. The sequential decline in Q4 reflected continued deposit repricing and competitive pressure for commercial deposits. The interest-earning asset yield was 5.31%, up 97 basis points from FY 2022. The total funding cost was 2.63%, up 78 basis points.

INTEREST RATE SENSITIVITY
Based on the Company's asset-liability models as of December 31, 2023, the estimated impact of parallel rate shifts on NII over the subsequent twelve months is as follows:
- Up 100 basis points: +$1.8 billion (+6.1%)
- Up 50 basis points: +$0.9 billion (+3.0%)
- Down 50 basis points: -$1.1 billion (-3.7%)
- Down 100 basis points: -$2.4 billion (-8.1%)

The Company is modestly asset-sensitive, with approximately $328 billion of floating-rate loans and $142 billion of floating-rate securities repricing within twelve months. Management utilizes interest rate swaps with a notional value of $86.4 billion to manage duration and convexity risk. The economic value of equity (EVE) sensitivity to a +200 basis point shock is estimated at -4.7%, within the Company's risk appetite of -8.0%.
